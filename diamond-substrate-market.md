<p><p>The diamond substrate market consists primarily of two types: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) substrates and High-Pressure High-Temperature (HPHT) diamond substrates. CVD substrates are created by depositing carbon atoms onto a substrate, producing high-quality synthetic diamonds with excellent uniformity, making them ideal for electronics and optics. In contrast, HPHT diamond substrates are formed by mimicking natural diamond formation conditions, resulting in robust and cost-effective diamonds suitable for industrial applications. Both types cater to diverse sectors, including electronics, jewelry, and cutting tools.</p></p>
