<p><ul><li>Polyetheretherketone</li><li>Titanium</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The spinal cage market comprises two primary material types: polyetheretherketone (PEEK) and titanium. PEEK is favored for its lightweight, biocompatibility, and flexibility, offering excellent imaging properties and reduced thermal conductivity. In contrast, titanium is known for its strength, durability, and excellent biocompatibility, providing a robust option for spinal fusion. Both materials serve different clinical needs, with PEEK often used in less invasive procedures and titanium preferred for its mechanical stability in more complex spinal surgeries.</p></p>
